My Missions Trip in Chicago

Reflections on my 2-month journey this summer

Introduction

WOW!!! What a summer it has been! After so much hesitation and deliberation, I decided to take 2 months of my summer to dedicate to a missions trip in Chicago. Alongside 43 other amazing students and a fair number of Cru staff, we shared the gospel message, fed and prayed for those in need, and overall just cared for people in Chicago whether we knew them or not.

It was such a blessing to be able to go on this trip. God worked in so many many ways, both in us and those around us. When there is a greenhouse of growth it's hard to miss how drastic someone can change in just 2 months, myself included. I am so glad I said yes to this, because I would have missed out on so many things God wanted to do in my life!

Below is a synopsis of what happened during the trip!!

Week 1: Arrival and First Impressions

We started off with all 44 students (myself included) arriving to our apartment complex. I knew a total of 2 people going into this trip, so being slightly introverted, it's safe to say that I was very intimidated and worried about how this would go. They had everyone get to know each other with a speed friending game, then sent everyone to do some bonding questions with their roommates (mine are pictured below).

This first week was mainly just getting acclimated to all the people, the groups (like which folks were in your Bible study), and the city itself (and how to use public transportation). There was a lot of information thrown at us in such a short period of time, but not once did I ever feel out of place or uncomfortable. The Lord gave me a peace that I could never make sense of, but it was something that I probably couldn't have done without.

This week also consisted of me settling into my work. I ended up working on two programming project through University of Detroit Mercy. I did this so that I could work remotely and get used to the lifestyle that would bring me. This led to me finding coffee shops to work at and my all time favorite spot the University of Illinois Chicago library. I worked on these projects Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, and Friday until the evenings. The evenings are for Cru events: training, community events, outreach (which I took off work for on Thursdays), and night with the Lord.

Week 2-4

This time was filled with learning and practicing. The Cru staff were pouring into us emotionally, spiritually, intellectually, and physically trying to ensure that we were as prepared as possible to take their place. The first month had huge emphasis on preparation and it certainly did prepare! One of the biggest struggles I faced in this month was setting aside the UofM vs. OSU rivalry and by doing that I made several life long friends that are just as passionate about following God's will for their lives as I was.

The Students Take Over

The staff left. Seemingly entrusting the entirety of the mission to 44 college students that had no idea what they were doing. When in reality, they left, entrusting 44 college students that trusted God and were actually excited to take over and freely give everything we had to God for the next month. This was one of the most bizzarre experiences of my entire life. In this month I watched people I met a month ago become a family, as we loved, ate with, cried with, struggled with, disagreed with, prayed with, and trusted eachother. This was one of the most God-centered communities I have ever been a part of. Everyone was assigned their role to take over and each person stepped into it ready to see how God wanted to use them. The most powerful part of this, was watching people acknowledge their roles and decide to lead by serving... the way Jesus taught us to. It felt like the staff were holding us back, because after they left it felt like an explosion went off. Now that I think about it, they were just preparing us and pouring fuel on that flame, so that when they left we would all be ignited again to make it our own and listen to how God wants us to do his work in Chicago.

Conclusion

This trip was a once in a lifetime experience. I made many lifelong friends, grew in almost every aspect (spiritually, emotionally, intellectually, relationally, etc.), and learned what it truly means to share the gospel with others. Instead of pushing our beliefs down poeple's throats, we focused on connection and SHOWING them what a God-Centered community looks like and then inviting them to join us. Yes we did share the gospel, but our focus was on having a conversation, while guided, showed people that we were genuinely interested in their stories.

Cool Stories

"Therefore my dear brothers and sisters, stand firm. Let nothing move you. Always give yourselves fully to the work of the Lord, for you know that your labor in him is not in vain" - 1 Corinthians 15:58

We stayed at Tailor Loft Apartments, which had front desk security 24/7. We got to know each of the front desk workers very well in 8.5 weeks, to the point that it sucked saying goodbye to them as well. However there were two specific interactions with them that I want to highlight.

First being with the front desk worker, Tony. Our group just got done with our Ice Cream Social that we invited the entire Lofts to and also everyone we met during outreach. I was headed up to my room to do some quiet time before bed, when Tony calls out "Hey, are you guys going to clean up in there?". I turned and said "Of course! We have people assigned to clean up, so don't worry we won't leave a mess". He then added after looking at the cameras "Would you look at that, they are already getting started. I was never worried, I know you guys, you are really responsible and we love having you here.". At this point I had gone over to the desk and we talked for a good 30 minutes. In that conversation he said something along the lines of "Your group is so different than all the other college students I experience. You are always so happy and laughing together." I took that as an opportunity to let him know that it was because of God that we can be like that. Which led to Tony and me trading stories about the different ways we have seen/experienced God working in our lives.

The second being with the front desk worker named Marquis. From week 1 I would always greet him with a fist bump and everytime our group came by a smile would come to his face. He always enjoyed talking to our group and he would ask a lot of questions. However, whenever we tried to guide the conversation towards faith or the gospel, he would start to shut it down. God worked so much in him that in the last two weeks he started asking questions about Christianity and about what we were doing in Chicago. I found out that he was muslim, but that he didn't really feel like he was "vibing" with it for lack of better terms. I was more than happy to talk to him about our group, what we believe, and what / why we were doing in Chicago.

One of the other really neat ways I saw God work was when my friend, Colton, and I were walking to our Wednesday community event. I had asked Colton how outreach went that day and in his resonse he was saying "Jesus" a lot. This lady had at some point been walking in the same direction as our group and I saw her look back at us, smile, and then just shake her head. I thought she was thinking "Oh, more of these Christian people again", and was a little unsettled at her display of disapproval. Then we stopped at a crosswalk and she turns back to us and says "I'm sorry to interrupt, but I just got off work. I have had a really long day, and I was looking forward to going home and doing nothing for the rest of the evening. My church is in their 'Holy Spirit' week, and I would typically go but I just feel so exhausted. So I prayed to God asking him to *in an exaggerated manner* give me a sign. As soon as I said Amen to that prayer, you guys started talking. I was like 'Okay God... FINE, I get it'". So then she thanked us for allowing God to use us even though we didn't know how it would happen, and she turned off to go to church.

I could go on for awhile about all of the really cool ways God worked in Chicago. But I don't think I would ever get around to sending this out to people if I did that. So if you are interested in hearing more about that or just have questions about my trip text me at (734) 635-1084
