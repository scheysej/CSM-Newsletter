My Missions Trip in Chicago

Reflections on my 2-month journey this summer

Introduction

WOW!!! What a summer it has been! After so much hesitation and deliberation, I decided to take 2 months of my summer to dedicate to a missions trip in Chicago. Alongside 43 other amazing students and a fair number of Cru staff, we shared the gospel message, fed and prayed for those in need, and overall just cared for people in Chicago whether we knew them or not.

It was such a blessing to be able to go on this trip. God worked in so many many ways, both in us and those around us. When there is a greenhouse of growth it's hard to miss how drastic someone can change in just 2 months, myself included. I am so glad I said yes to this, because I would have missed out on so many things God wanted to do in my life!

Below is a synopsis of what happened during the trip!!

Week 1: Arrival and First Impressions

We started off with all 44 students (myself included) arriving to our apartment complex. I knew a total of 2 people going into this trip, so being slightly introverted, it's safe to say that I was very intimidated and worried about how this would go. They had everyone get to know each other with a speed friending game, then sent everyone to do some bonding questions with their roommates (mine are pictured below).

This first week was mainly just getting acclimated to all the people, the groups (like which folks were in your Bible study), and the city itself (and how to use public transportation). There was a lot of information thrown at us in such a short period of time, but not once did I ever feel out of place or uncomfortable. The Lord gave me a peace that I could never make sense of, but it was something that I probably couldn't have done without.

This week also consisted of me settling into my work. I ended up working on two programming project through University of Detroit Mercy. I did this so that I could work remotely and get used to the lifestyle that would bring me. This led to me finding coffee shops to work at and my all time favorite spot the University of Illinois Chicago library. I worked on these projects Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, and Friday until the evenings. The evenings are for Cru events: training, community events, outreach (which I did instead of work on Thursdays), and night with the Lord.

Week 2-4

This time was filled with learning and practicing. The Cru staff were pouring into us emotionally, spiritually, intellectually, and physically trying to ensure that we were as prepared as possible to take their place. The first month had huge emphasis on preparation and it certainly did prepare! One of the biggest struggles I faced in this month was setting aside the UofM vs. OSU rivalry and by doing that I made several life long friends that are just as passionate about following God's will for their lives as I was.

The Students Take Over

The staff left. Seemingly entrusting the entirety of the mission to 44 college students that had no idea what they were doing. When in reality, they left, entrusting 44 college students that trusted God and were actually excited to take over and freely give everything we had to God for the next month. This was one of the most bizzarre experiences of my entire life. In this month I watched people I met a month ago become a family, as we loved, ate with, cried with, struggled with, disagreed with, prayed with, and trusted eachother. This was one of the most God-centered communities I have ever been a part of. Everyone was assigned their role to take over and each person stepped into it ready to see how God wanted to use them. The most powerful part of this, was watching people acknowledge their roles and decide to lead by serving... the way Jesus taught us to. It felt like the staff were holding us back, because after they left it felt like an explosion went off. Now that I think about it, they were just preparing us and pouring fuel on that fire, so that when they left we would all be ignited again to make it our own and listen to how God wants us to do his work in Chicago.

Conclusion

As my trip came to an end, I reflected on all that I had learned and the connections I had made. This missions trip was a transformative experience that I will cherish for a lifetime. I am grateful for the chance to serve and grow in such a vibrant city.
